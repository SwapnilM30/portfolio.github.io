A Curious Journey into Robotics

At the age of 14, I became fascinated with how infants learn by observing the world around them as they grow. This sparked a question in my mind: Can machines also perceive the world with such curiosity?

Motivated by this curiosity, I discovered the field of robotics and was immediately captivated by the exciting research and work being done. From that moment on, I knew I was destined to become a robotics engineer. Alongside my regular college courses, I tirelessly worked to develop robot circuitry and create various types of robots. This hands-on experience further fueled my interest in the field and solidified my determination to fully immerse myself in the captivating world of robotics. Driven by a thirst for knowledge, I embarked on a journey to explore the intricate domains of robotics and artificial intelligence.

My early fascination with robotics led me to pursue a graduate degree in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at Arizona State University. Exploring the intricate domains of robotics and artificial intelligence has been a thoroughly enjoyable experience, and I eagerly seize every opportunity to learn more in this ever-evolving and dynamic field. My journey to become a robotics engineer has been incredible so far, and I am excited to see where it takes me in the future.

PCB for Manual and Autonomous Robots

Problem Statement and Solution:

Team Vector, our college's robotics club, encountered a challenge during our participation in ABU Robocon-India, where we had to connect several electronic subsystems using a large number of wires. This led to loose connections, confusion, and EMI/EMC issues during the electrical rack assembly and debugging process. However, we overcame this obstacle by devising a PCB that incorporated all the necessary functionalities and interfaces commonly used in the competition.

Battery Fuel Gauge

Problem Statement and Solution:

Team Vector's testing team always had to remove the electrical rack of the robot to check the battery pack's health with the help of a battery checker. This was a repetitive and cumbersome task. However, with the introduction of the 'Battery Fuel Gauge' system, we had a clear display of the robot's battery pack health through an onboard LED array. Additionally, we received an alert message when the battery charge dropped below a particular limit.

LCD & Relay Breakout Board

Problem Statement and Solution:

With the integration of the 'Battery Fuel Gauge' system, Team Vector wanted to have an LCD display outside the robot that would show the status of the battery pack. In addition to displaying the battery status, the LCD display is versatile and can be used for a variety of purposes. The breakout board also includes relays, making this combination suitable for a wide range of applications.

Retro-converted Electric Scooter

Problem Statement and Solution:

One of my friends had a petrol-driven Honda Aviator, which he was planning to sell at a lower price since he was not using it due to reduced mileage. One day, we had an idea: to convert this petrol-driven scooter into a fully-fledged electric scooter. In search of components, we scoured the local market and were fortunate enough to stumble upon an abandoned electric two-wheeler. This discovery led us to a new plan: retro-converting the petrol-driven Honda Aviator into an electric scooter.
