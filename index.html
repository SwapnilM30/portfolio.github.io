Explore with Me: My Tech Odyssey

Hey there, I’m Swapnil Meshram, an Electronics Engineer who’s deeply invested in robotics. Right now, I’m working on my Master’s in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at Arizona State University, and it’s been an exciting ride so far.

My journey into robotics has been all about diving into the intricacies of how robots think, move, and interact with their environment. I’m fascinated by the challenges of autonomy, control, and mechanics, and I’m always looking to push the limits of what’s possible. At the same time, I stay grounded in my roots—electronics engineering and hardware design, which are at the core of everything I do.

This portfolio is more than just a showcase of my work; it’s a snapshot of my journey through robotics and electronics. From designing intricate hardware to building intelligent systems, each project reflects my commitment to both fields.

Join me as we explore this journey together and witness the evolution of my work. Welcome aboard!

    A Curious Journey into Robotics

    At 14, I became captivated by how infants learn through observing their surroundings. This sparked a profound question: Can machines also perceive the world with the same curiosity?

    Driven by this thought, I discovered robotics—a field that immediately captivated me with its potential and ongoing innovations. From that point, I dedicated myself to becoming a robotics engineer. Beyond my college coursework, I immersed myself in hands-on projects, developing robot circuitry and creating various types of robots. These experiences not only deepened my fascination but also solidified my resolve to dive deeper into robotics and artificial intelligence.

    My early fascination has since evolved into a dedicated academic pursuit. Currently, I am pursuing a master’s degree in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at Arizona State University, where each day presents new challenges and opportunities to learn. The journey has been incredible, and I am excited to see where this passion for robotics will lead me next.

    My Projects

    Comparative Analysis of SLAM Algorithms

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    This project investigates the performance of two prominent SLAM algorithms, GMapping and Cartographer, using the Yahboom ROS Master X3 robot. By assessing their mapping accuracy and computational efficiency in various indoor settings, we address identified limitations by proposing the integration of additional sensors and depth cameras to enhance obstacle detection, ultimately improving SLAM accuracy and reliability in diverse environments.

    Project details

    EdgeVision: User Defined Object-Counting

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    This project aims to develop a machine learning toolbox capable of detecting and tracking objects in video streams. The toolbox includes an interactive feature that allows users to draw line segments on the video, which automatically counts the number of objects passing through these lines. Key features of the toolbox include object detection, tracking of detected objects, and the interactive tool for drawing line segments that facilitate real-time object counting.

    Project details

    PCB for Manual and Autonomous Robots

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    Team Vector, our college's robotics club, encountered a challenge during our participation in ABU Robocon-India, where we had to connect several electronic subsystems using a large number of wires. This led to loose connections, confusion, and EMI/EMC issues during the electrical rack assembly and debugging process. However, we overcame this obstacle by devising a PCB that incorporated all the necessary functionalities and interfaces commonly used in the competition.

    Project details

    Battery Fuel Gauge

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    Team Vector's testing team always had to remove the electrical rack of the robot to check the battery pack's health with the help of a battery checker. This was a repetitive and cumbersome task. However, with the introduction of the 'Battery Fuel Gauge' system, we had a clear display of the robot's battery pack health through an onboard LED array. Additionally, we received an alert message when the battery charge dropped below a particular limit.

    Project details

    LCD & Relay Breakout Board

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    With the integration of the 'Battery Fuel Gauge' system, Team Vector wanted to have an LCD display outside the robot that would show the status of the battery pack. In addition to displaying the battery status, the LCD display is versatile and can be used for a variety of purposes. The breakout board also includes relays, making this combination suitable for a wide range of applications.

    Multipurpose PCB Ruler

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    During the PCB design process, we usually find ourselves constantly searching for PCB-related calculations and component footprints online, both during the design and debugging of the boards. As a solution, the PCB Ruler simplifies these issues by integrating all essential PCB calculations and component footprints, making design and debugging more efficient than ever. Say goodbye to endless web searches - the answers are now at your fingertips with our multipurpose PCB ruler.

    Retro-converted Electric Scooter

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    One of my friends had a petrol-driven Honda Aviator, which he was planning to sell at a lower price since he was not using it due to reduced mileage. One day, we had an idea: to convert this petrol-driven scooter into a fully-fledged electric scooter. In search of components, we scoured the local market and were fortunate enough to stumble upon an abandoned electric two-wheeler. This discovery led us to a new plan: retro-converting the petrol-driven Honda Aviator into an electric scooter.
