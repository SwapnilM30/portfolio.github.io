During my tenure in the Autonomous & Undersea Systems Division at Aerospace Engineers Private Limited (AEPL), I led the research and development of marine robotic vehicles, resulting in significant cost savings and improved project management within the Electrical & Electronics department.

My responsibilities involved designing complex electrical frameworks for various unmanned marine robotic vehicles. This included the development of a 2.15-meter-long Autonomous Underwater Vehicle (AUV) with an endurance of nearly 4 hours, capable of operating at depths of up to 300 meters. The portfolio also comprised Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROVs) and Autonomous Surface Vessels (ASVs).

My role encompassed the entire lifecycle of marine robotic vehicle development. I led the design process from inception to functional prototypes, including hardware selection, schematic and PCB design, board bring-up, and system-level integration. My collaboration with cross-functional teams allowed for the integration of custom-designed hardware, underwater sensors and payloads, and communication systems, resulting in the successful creation of marine robotic vehicles.

Additionally, I facilitated collaboration throughout the product development lifecycle, including design, review, testing, debugging, and thorough documentation. This ensured that all aspects of the project were meticulously recorded and documented for future reference and improvement.
