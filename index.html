Explore with Me: My Tech Odyssey

Hey there! I am Swapnil Meshram, an electronics engineer with a passion for robotics. I am pursuing my M.S. in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at Arizona State University, and I love how each project and coursework pushes me to learn something new.

My journey into robotics has been centered on understanding how robots think, move, and interact with their surroundings. I am fascinated by autonomy, control systems, and mechanical challenges, and I thrive on pushing the boundaries of what these machines can do. I draw on my electronics engineering and hardware-design expertise to build robust, high-performing systems.

This portfolio is not just a showcase of my work. It is a look at how I have grown in robotics and electronics. Each project, from designing intricate hardware to developing intelligent systems, demonstrates my dedication to both fields.

Take a look around to see how I tackle real-world engineering challenges with curiosity and creativity. Welcome aboard!

    A Curious Journey into Robotics

    When I was 14, I became captivated by how infants learn simply by observing the world around them. It left me wondering if machines could develop a similar curiosity and ability to learn from their surroundings. That question sparked a fascination that grew into a deep passion for robotics and machine learning.

    During my undergraduate years, I turned this curiosity into action by designing robot circuitry, writing embedded firmware, and building robots to tackle sophisticated tasks. And nothing felt more rewarding than watching an idea I have poured hours into come to life. Those hands-on experiences only deepened my fascination and solidified my resolve to dive further into the field of robotics and artificial intelligence.

    My early fascination has since evolved into a dedicated academic pursuit. Currently, I am pursuing a master’s degree in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at Arizona State University, where each day presents new challenges and opportunities to learn. The journey has been incredible, and I am excited to see where this passion for robotics will lead me next.

    Comparative Analysis of SLAM Algorithms

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    This project investigates the performance of two prominent SLAM algorithms, GMapping and Cartographer, using the Yahboom ROS Master X3 robot. By assessing their mapping accuracy and computational efficiency in various indoor settings, we identified key limitations and proposed integrating additional sensors and depth cameras. This integration aims to enhance obstacle detection capabilities, thereby improving SLAM accuracy and reliability in diverse, complex environments.

    EdgeVision: User Defined Object-Counting using Raspberry Pi

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    This project aims to develop a machine learning toolbox that detects and tracks objects in video streams. The toolbox includes an interactive feature that allows users to draw line segments directly on the video feed, automatically counting the objects passing through these lines. Core functionalities include real-time object detection, continuous object tracking, and interactive user-defined counters for dynamic analytics.

    PCB for Manual and Autonomous Robots

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    Team Vector, our college's robotics club, encountered a challenge during our participation in ABU Robocon-India, where we had to connect several electronic subsystems using a large number of wires.     Battery Fuel Gauge

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    Team Vector's testing team always had to remove the electrical rack of the robot to check the battery pack's health with the help of a battery checker. This was a repetitive and cumbersome task. However, with the introduction of the 'Battery Fuel Gauge' system, we had a clear display of the robot's battery pack health through an onboard LED array. Additionally, we received an alert message when the battery charge dropped below a particular limit.

    LCD & Relay Breakout Board

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    With the integration of the 'Battery Fuel Gauge' system, Team Vector wanted to have an LCD display outside the robot that would show the status of the battery pack.     Retro-converted Electric Scooter

    Problem Statement and Solution:

    One of my friends had a petrol-driven Honda Aviator, which he was planning to sell at a lower price since he was not using it due to reduced mileage. One day, we had an idea: to convert this petrol-driven scooter into a fully-fledged electric scooter. In search of components, we scoured the local market and were fortunate enough to stumble upon an abandoned electric two-wheeler. This discovery led us to a new plan: retro-converting the petrol-driven Honda Aviator into an electric scooter.
